# Stakeholders

## 1. Purpose

This document identifies the key stakeholders involved in or affected by the Enterprise Network Transformation project. It captures their responsibilities, concerns, expectations, and influence on the architecture.

---

## 2. Stakeholder Overview

| Stakeholder | Role | Primary Concern | Influence |
|---|---|---|---|
| CIO / IT Leadership | Executive Sponsor | Business value and investment | High |
| CISO / Security Team | Security Governance | Risk and compliance | High |
| Network Architecture Team | Architecture Design | Technical quality and scalability | High |
| Network Operations Team | Network Operations | Stability and manageability | High |
| Infrastructure Team | Infrastructure Services | Platform integration | Medium |
| Application Teams | Application Owners | Application connectivity | Medium |
| Service Desk | Incident Management | Troubleshooting and support | Medium |
| End Users | Network Consumers | Performance and availability | Medium |
| Finance / Procurement | Financial Governance | Cost and licensing | Medium |
| Vendors | Technology Providers | Solution delivery and support | Medium |

---

## 3. Stakeholder Concerns

### CIO / IT Leadership

- Business value
- Return on investment
- Transformation timeline
- Risk reduction
- Operational efficiency

### CISO / Security Team

- Identity-based access control
- Network segmentation
- Security policy enforcement
- Compliance
- Auditability

### Network Operations Team

- Operational simplicity
- Monitoring and visibility
- Troubleshooting
- Automation
- Maintainability

### Application Teams

- Application availability
- Network performance
- Latency
- Connectivity dependencies
- Migration impact

### End Users

- Reliable connectivity
- Network performance
- Minimal disruption
- Secure access

---

| Stakeholder | Influence | Interest | Engagement Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| CIO / IT Leadership | High | High | Executive reviews |

| Network Operations | High | High | Technical workshops |
